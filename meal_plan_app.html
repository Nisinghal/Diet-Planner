Health Guidelines

💪

Workout Nutrition — Fat Loss & ToningGym

For 62kg, 155cm — 3-4 gym sessions/week
  • Pre-workout (1hr before): banana + 4 soaked almonds or sattu drink — quick carbs + protein
  • Post-workout (within 30 min): paneer 50g or moong dal cheela — repair & build muscle
  • Gym days: eat 1,500 kcal; rest days: 1,300 kcal — cycle calories for better results
  • Protein goal 80–90g/day: spread across all meals, not just one sitting
  • Strength train 3-4×/wk + walk 7,000 steps daily = optimal fat loss + toning combo
  • Don't skip meals on gym days — thyroid slows metabolism further when under-fuelled
  • Post-dinner haldi milk aids muscle recovery and reduces cortisol spike from training
🌿

Eat More — Thyroid & AutoimmuneThyroidHashimoto's

  • Selenium: sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds — supports T4→T3 conversion
  • Zinc: moong dal, chickpeas, pumpkin seeds — thyroid hormone synthesis
  • Anti-inflammatory: haldi (daily ✓), walnut, ginger, flaxseed
  • Gut-health: curd, chanch, kadhi — reduces autoimmune activity
  • Iron + B12: ragi, spinach, legumes — fight fatigue (common in hypothyroid)
  • Magnesium: almonds, leafy greens — reduces muscle cramps & fatigue
  • Vitamin D: morning sun 15 min + doctor-guided supplement
🚫

Avoid / Limit for Thyroid

  • Raw gobhi, broccoli, kale — always cook; cooking destroys goitrogens
  • Soy in excess — blocks thyroid hormone uptake; space 4 hr from medication
  • Gluten (for Hashimoto's) — your plan uses gluten-free bread ✓
  • Refined sugar, maida — spikes cortisol, worsens inflammation
  • Coffee/tea within 60 min of thyroid meds — impairs absorption
  • Alcohol — impairs T4→T3 conversion and disrupts sleep
💇

Hair Growth & AlopeciaHair

  • Iron is critical — low ferritin = hair loss; dal + spinach + ragi daily
  • Vitamin C with iron foods — lemon/carrot with dal boosts iron absorption 3×
  • Biotin: almonds, eggs, sweet potato — keratin structure support
  • Silica: oats, cucumber — strengthens hair shaft
  • Collagen precursors: amla, lemon, chickpeas — natural collagen formation
  • Hydration — dehydration directly stresses hair follicles; hit 10 glasses ✓
  • Scalp: coconut/brahmi oil massage 2× week; avoid SLS shampoos

Seasonal Swaps for Hard-to-Find Ingredients

  • Blueberries → Jamun (June–Aug) or Anar — same anthocyanins, thyroid-protective
  • Avocado → Banana + walnuts — similar healthy fat profile, easier to source
  • Strawberry → Amla or Guava — 3× more Vit C, anti-inflammatory, widely available
  • Greek yoghurt → Hung curd (chakka dahi) — same protein, made at home
  • Kale → Palak or Methi — equal iron + folate, year-round Indian availability
  • Quinoa → Ragi or Foxtail millet (Kangni) — same protein, traditionally Indian
🧪

Stomach Acid & Digestion — The Missing LinkThyroid

  • Chew each bite 20–30 times — mechanical chewing triggers stomach acid production
  • Lemon water 10 min before meals — stimulates HCl, prepares gut for nutrient absorption
  • Low stomach acid = poor absorption of iodine, selenium, zinc, B-vitamins — all critical for T4→T3
  • Signs of low acid: bloating after meals, heartburn, food sensitivities, persistent iron/B12 deficiency
  • Prebiotic foods daily: garlic, onions, methi, high-fiber vegetables like bhindi and tinda
  • Avoid processed/packaged foods — deplete stomach acid over time, worsen thyroid function
🧠

Mind–Gut–Thyroid AxisHashimoto's

  • Stress → cortisol spike → shuts digestion → disrupts gut bacteria → leaky gut → Hashimoto's flares
  • Gut produces 90% of serotonin; unhealthy gut = low mood, anxiety, brain fog with thyroid
  • 5–10 min deep breathing before meals activates parasympathetic system, improves absorption
  • Journaling/emotional processing — unresolved stress directly manifests as thyroid and gut symptoms
  • Avoid overexercising — HIIT spikes cortisol, harms gut and thyroid; walks + yoga preferred
  • No screens 30 min before bed — blue light disrupts melatonin, raises cortisol overnight
  • Limit caffeine after noon — overstimulates nervous system, disrupts healthy gut bacteria balance
📋

Common Myths — Know the Facts

  • Myth: TSH test alone is enough. Fact: Insist on free T3, free T4, and thyroid antibodies (TPO/TgAb) for complete picture
  • Myth: Iodine supplements help hypothyroidism. Fact: Excess iodine worsens Hashimoto's — get it only from food (salt, dairy)
  • Myth: Hypothyroid only causes weight gain. Fact: Also hair loss, depression, muscle weakness, cold sensitivity, constipation, skin issues
  • Myth: You must take medication forever. Fact: Many reduce dose by fixing root causes (stress, diet, gut) under doctor's guidance
  • Myth: Herbs fully cure thyroid. Fact: Ashwagandha/guggul support function but cannot replace thyroid hormone replacement
  • Myth: Only women get it. Fact: Men and children get it too; men are significantly underdiagnosed
  • Myth: Diet alone cures thyroid. Fact: Diet is one pillar; stress management, sleep, gut health, and movement are equally essential
